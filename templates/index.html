P2M Project

AI based Failure Prediction of 4G Network Base Station

Context

  • Problem
  • Data
  • Expectations

Problem Description

Companies strive to develop methods that allow the identification of eventual failures before they occur.
In this project, we will address the challenges encountered by telecom operators and propose AI- based techniques to identify, predict and notify the occurrence of failure events.

Our Objective

The purpose is to build an AI model to predict future Radio Link failure (RLF) occurrences through Radio Link KPI history and its associated weather forecast data as inputs to produce actionable information. Which will benefit maintenance personnel to deal with faults in advance and effectively avoid the base station from out-of-service.

The dataset provided contains anonymyzed information associated to meteorological stations and RF-links, comprising:

Meteo forecast

Station Id - Data Time - Weather

RL KPis

Site Id - Date Time - Severely errored second - Error second - Unavail second - Avail time - Bbe - RLF

Distances

Distances between Sites and Stations.

Data Quality

Our model is as good as the weather predictions. The source of the weather forecasts, their accuracy, and consistency are the most important characteristics exploited by our model.

Our Model

Our experiments and discussions supported the use of ‘Random forest classifier’ as the preferred model in order to:
• Maximize interpretability.
• Make our model robust against feature scaling issues.
• Use the embedded feature importance capability for dimensionality reduction.
For simplicity when building our model:
The weather forecast used is the next day’s only, and belongs to the closest meteorological station to the RF site of interest.
